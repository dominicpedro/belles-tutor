## 24-Hour Time -- The "+12 Trick"

Here's the secret: if it's afternoon/evening/night, just **add 12**. That's it!

### The Rule

| Time of day | How to convert | Example |
|-------------|---------------|---------|
| **Morning (am)** | Keep the hour, write two digits | 7:20 am = **07:20** |
| **Afternoon/Evening (pm)** | **Add 12** to the hour | 1:20 pm = **13:20** |
| **Midnight** | = **00:00** | 12:00 am = **00:00** |
| **Noon** | = **12:00** | 12:00 pm = **12:00** |

### Step-by-Step Method

1. Is it **am** or **pm**?
2. If **am**: write the hour with a leading zero if needed (7 becomes 07).
3. If **pm**: **add 12** to the hour. Then write the minutes.
4. Double-check: is your answer between 12:00 and 23:59 for pm times?

### Worked Example

> "A quarter to 9 in the evening"
>
> 1. "Quarter to 9" = 8:45 (15 minutes before 9)
> 2. "In the evening" = pm
> 3. PM so add 12: 8 + 12 = **20**
> 4. Answer: **20:45**

> **Watch out!** You wrote 08:45 instead of 20:45 for this question. You forgot to add 12! Every time you see "afternoon," "evening," or "night," you MUST add 12.

> **Watch out!** You wrote 01:20 for "20 minutes past 7 in the morning." The correct answer is **07:20**. For morning times, the hour stays the same -- just make sure you write two digits.

### Converting Back (24-hour to 12-hour)

- If the hour is **less than 12**: it is **am**. Write the hour as-is.
- If the hour is **12 or more**: **subtract 12** and label **pm**.
  - 14:25 --> 14 - 12 = 2 --> **2:25 pm**
  - 22:30 --> 22 - 12 = 10 --> **10:30 pm**

## Elapsed Time -- Your Secret Weapon: The Timeline

This is the trickiest part, but there's one method that works EVERY time: **draw a timeline and jump in chunks**.

### Step-by-Step Method: Count Forward in Chunks

1. **Draw a timeline** with the start time on the left and end time on the right.
2. **Jump to the next full hour** first. Write down how many minutes that took.
3. **Count the full hours** from there to the last full hour before the end time.
4. **Add the leftover minutes** to reach the end time.
5. **Add up** all the chunks.

### Worked Example 1: Same part of the day

> How much time from **10:24** to **11:20**?

```
10:24 ---[36 min]---> 11:00 ---[20 min]---> 11:20
```

- 10:24 to 11:00 = 60 - 24 = **36 minutes**
- 11:00 to 11:20 = **20 minutes**
- Total = 36 + 20 = **56 minutes**

> **Watch out!** You wrote 50 minutes for this question. Always draw the timeline and add the chunks carefully.

### Worked Example 2: Crossing from AM to PM

> How much time from **7:45 am** to **2:35 pm**?

```
7:45 am --[15 min]--> 8:00 am --[4 h]--> 12:00 pm --[2 h]--> 2:00 pm --[35 min]--> 2:35 pm
```

- 7:45 to 8:00 = **15 minutes**
- 8:00 to 12:00 = **4 hours**
- 12:00 to 2:00 = **2 hours**
- 2:00 to 2:35 = **35 minutes**
- Total = 4 + 2 = 6 hours, and 15 + 35 = 50 minutes --> **6 hours 50 minutes**

> **Watch out!** You wrote 2 hours for this question. When the start is am and the end is pm, you MUST count through 12:00 noon. Do not just subtract the hour numbers.

### Worked Example 3: Crossing Midnight

> How much time from **21:00** to **08:30**?

```
21:00 ---[3 h]---> 00:00 (midnight) ---[8 h]---> 08:00 ---[30 min]---> 08:30
```

- 21:00 to 00:00 = 24 - 21 = **3 hours**
- 00:00 to 08:00 = **8 hours**
- 08:00 to 08:30 = **30 minutes**
- Total = 3 + 8 = **11 hours 30 minutes**

> **Watch out!** You wrote 5 hours 30 minutes for this question. When the end time is a smaller number than the start time in 24-hour format (08:30 is less than 21:00), the time crosses midnight. Count to midnight first (24:00 - 21:00 = 3 hours), then from midnight to the end.

### Worked Example 4: Repeated Intervals

> A nurse checks her patient every 45 minutes, starting at 11:00 am. What are the next 4 visit times?

```
11:00 --[+45]--> 11:45 --[+45]--> 12:30 --[+45]--> 1:15 --[+45]--> 2:00
```

- 11:00 + 45 min = **11:45 am**
- 11:45 + 45 min = 11:45 + 15 = 12:00, then + 30 = **12:30 pm**
- 12:30 + 45 min = 12:30 + 30 = 1:00, then + 15 = **1:15 pm**
- 1:15 + 45 min = 1:15 + 45 = **2:00 pm**

**Tip for adding minutes past the hour:** Split the minutes. If you are at 11:45 and need to add 45, first add enough to reach the next hour (15 minutes gets you to 12:00), then add what is left (45 - 15 = 30 minutes).

> **Watch out!** You wrote 12:25 instead of 12:30 for the second visit. When you add 45 to 11:45, you cross the hour. Split it: 15 minutes to reach 12:00, then 30 minutes more = 12:30.

## Time Subtraction -- Borrow 60, Not 10!

Here's the thing that catches everyone: time doesn't work like normal subtraction. When you borrow, you get **60** (not 10 like in normal maths). This is because there are 60 seconds in a minute and 60 minutes in an hour.

### The Rule

- If you cannot subtract the seconds, **borrow 1 minute = 60 seconds**.
- If you cannot subtract the minutes, **borrow 1 hour = 60 minutes**.

### Step-by-Step Method

1. Write the times in columns: hours | minutes | seconds.
2. Start from the right (seconds first).
3. If the top number is smaller, **borrow 60** from the next column.
4. Subtract each column.

### Worked Example: Time Difference with Borrowing

> Dan stands on his hands for 3 min 10 sec. Adila stands for 2 min 45 sec. How much longer is Dan?

```
  3 min  10 sec
- 2 min  45 sec
```

10 is less than 45. You cannot subtract. **Borrow 1 minute (= 60 seconds).**

```
  2 min  70 sec     (took 1 min from the 3, added 60 sec to the 10)
- 2 min  45 sec
= 0 min  25 sec
```

**Answer: 25 seconds**

> **Watch out!** You wrote 1 minute 35 seconds. You subtracted 10 from 45 (getting 35) instead of borrowing. ALWAYS check: is the top number big enough? If not, borrow 60.

### Worked Example: Swimming Time (hh:mm:ss)

> Sindi's friend swims 50 m. Start: 01:30:48. Finish: 01:31:25. How long?

```
  01 : 31 : 25
- 01 : 30 : 48
```

25 is less than 48. Borrow 1 minute (= 60 seconds).

```
  01 : 30 : 85     (took 1 min from 31, added 60 sec to 25)
- 01 : 30 : 48
= 00 : 00 : 37
```

**Answer: 37 seconds**

> **Watch out!** You wrote 1 minute 23 seconds. Remember to borrow from the minutes column when the seconds are not enough.

## Finding Start Times -- Work Backwards

Sometimes the question tells you WHEN something ended and HOW LONG it took, and you need to figure out when it started. You work backwards by subtracting.

### Step-by-Step Method

1. Write down the end time.
2. Subtract the hours first.
3. Subtract the minutes.
4. If the minutes go below zero, borrow 1 hour (= 60 minutes).

### Worked Example

> A show ends at 23:00. Total duration is 2 hours 50 minutes (including interval). What is the latest start time?

```
23:00
- 2 h 50 min
```

Step 1: 23:00 - 2 hours = 21:00
Step 2: 21:00 - 50 minutes --> 21:00 has 0 minutes, so borrow 1 hour:

```
20 : 60
-  0 : 50
= 20 : 10
```

**Answer: 20:10 (8:10 pm)**

> **Watch out!** You wrote 21:50 for this question. It looks like you subtracted only 1 hour 10 minutes instead of 2 hours 50 minutes. Read the question carefully -- the show is 2 h 20 min PLUS a 30-minute interval = 2 h 50 min total.

## Finding End Times -- Work Forwards

The question gives you a start time and tells you how long something takes. Just add!

### Step-by-Step Method

1. Write down the start time.
2. Add the hours first.
3. Add the minutes.
4. If the minutes go past 60, carry 1 hour.

### Worked Example

> Mrs Brown puts a chicken in the oven at 4:30 pm. It cooks for 1 hour 20 minutes. When does she take it out?

Step 1: 4:30 + 1 hour = 5:30
Step 2: 5:30 + 20 minutes = 5:50

**Answer: 5:50 pm**

> **Watch out!** You wrote 5:20 pm. Make sure you add the full duration: first add the hours, THEN add the minutes. Do not skip a step.

> How long from **2:40 pm** to **5:25 pm**?

```
2:40 pm --[20 min]--> 3:00 pm --[2 h]--> 5:00 pm --[25 min]--> 5:25 pm
```

- 2:40 to 3:00 = **20 minutes**
- 3:00 to 5:00 = **2 hours**
- 5:00 to 5:25 = **25 minutes**
