## Student Patterns (Quick Reference)

### Critical Weaknesses
- Elapsed time calculations (crossing hour/am-pm/midnight boundaries)
- 24-hour time conversion (forgetting +12 for PM)
- Division-based word problems (cannot connect multiples to division)
- Ratio/proportion beyond tables (cannot apply multiplicative relationship)

### Strong Areas
- Addition strategies (breaking up, rounding & compensating, pairing)
- Matchstick patterns and growing patterns
- Multiples identification and relationships
- Transformations on dot grids
- Data handling (bar graphs, dot plots, mode)
- Fractions on number lines and of money

### Misconception Types
1. **Boundary crossing:** Struggles when calculations cross a boundary (0,9 → 1,0; 59 min → next hour; AM → PM)
2. **Scale confusion:** Correct method for small numbers, breaks down for large (rounding 4-digit numbers, cost tables for hundreds)
3. **Procedure vs understanding:** Can follow a table/pattern but cannot apply the underlying relationship to new values
4. **Base-60 vs base-10:** Treats time arithmetic as base-10 (borrows 10 instead of 60)

## Learning Style Notes

- Timelines are the most effective scaffold for time problems
- Money contexts (Rands) make fractions and decimals concrete
- Can perform transformations physically but needs vocabulary practice
