## Observations

- **Timeline method is natural** — Belle described "jump to next hour, then count hours" before being taught it. She already uses breaking-up for addition — same instinct applies here.
- **Self-correction on 60-24** — Initially said 46 (thinking she needed to add 40), then caught herself: "you needed to plus 30 because 4+6=10 so you already have a ten." Shows she can reason about the arithmetic when slowed down.
- **AM/PM crossing handled** — Counted 8:00 am to 2:00 pm = 6 hours correctly. No confusion about the boundary.
- **Midnight crossing handled** — When prompted to break at midnight, she did 9pm→midnight = 3 hours, midnight→8:30 = 8h30, total 11h30 correctly.
- **Initial rush on 10:24→11:20** — Jumped to "2 hours 6 minutes" before being asked to slow down and use the method. When guided through steps, got it right. The method prevents errors but she needs to remember to USE it instead of guessing.

## Key Takeaway

Belle can do elapsed time correctly when she uses the timeline method step by step. Her workbook errors (2 hours instead of 6h50, 5h30 instead of 11h30) were caused by trying to do it in one jump. The method eliminates this.
