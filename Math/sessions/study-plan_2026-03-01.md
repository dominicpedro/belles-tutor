BELLE'S MATH TEST STUDY PLAN
Test: Tuesday 10 March
Total: 9 days | 15-20 min per day | 1 rest day

---

DAY 1 — Sunday 1 March (today)
Read the time quick card together, then a quick quiz on 24-hour time.
Focus: The "+12 for PM" rule (e.g. 3:45 pm = 15:45)
Time: 15 min
Tip: Keep it light — this is day one, not exam day.

DAY 2 — Monday 2 March
Study session on elapsed time — her biggest weakness.
Focus: How to count forward across hour boundaries using a timeline.
Time: 20 min (hard stop — even if going well, stop on a win)
Tip: If she gets frustrated, stop. "We'll pick this up tomorrow."

DAY 3 — Tuesday 3 March
Quick quiz on yesterday's elapsed time (5 min), then study division word problems (10 min).
Focus: Connecting "counting in 1 500s" to "how many can you buy?"
Time: 15 min total
Tip: Use money examples — "If tickets cost R1 500 and you have R12 000..."

DAY 4 — Wednesday 4 March
REST DAY. No studying. Let the brain consolidate.
Tip: Drop one "teach me" question casually at dinner (see below).

DAY 5 — Thursday 5 March
Mixed quiz across all weak areas.
Focus: 24-hour time, elapsed time, division — all mixed together.
Time: 15 min
Tip: This is a temperature check, not a cram session. Just note what's improved.

DAY 6 — Friday 6 March
Study session on whatever was still weak after Day 5.
Time: 15 min
Tip: If everything felt okay, do transformation vocabulary instead (translate = slide, reflect = flip, rotate = turn).

DAY 7 — Saturday 7 March
Full practice test — the dress rehearsal.
Time: 20 min
Tip: Don't mark it in front of her. Let her finish, then review together.

DAY 8 — Sunday 8 March
Review the practice test mistakes only. Quick study session on gaps.
Time: 15 min
Tip: "You got most of that right. Let's just clean up these last few."

DAY 9 — Monday 9 March (night before)
Confidence round — easy quiz on things she's GOOD at (addition, patterns, data handling).
Time: 10 min
Tip: End on "You've got this." No new material. No cramming.

TUESDAY 10 MARCH — TEST DAY
No morning studying. Breakfast, deep breath, go.

---

"TEACH ME" PROMPTS
(Use these anytime — car, dinner, walking the dog. Pretend YOU'RE the one who's confused and let her be the expert.)

"I need to figure out how long from 10:45 to 1:20... how do you even do that? Can you show me?"

"Wait — is 3 pm the same as 15:00 or 03:00? I can never remember."

"If tickets cost R1 500 each and I have R12 000... how many can I buy? I'm stuck."

"Someone told me you borrow 10 in time subtraction, same as normal maths. Is that right?"

"What's the difference between a reflection and a rotation? I always mix those up."

If she explains it well — react with genuine "oh, that makes sense!" energy.
If she struggles — don't correct her. Just say "hmm, let's check your notes together."

---

QUICK QUESTIONS
(Drop these into conversation whenever)

"What's 7:30 pm in 24-hour time?"
"How many minutes in 3/4 of an hour?"
"What do you borrow in time subtraction — 10 or 60?"
"4:30 pm plus 1 hour 20 minutes — what time?"
"What's the mode of 3, 5, 5, 7, 5, 2?"

---

IMPORTANT RULES

- If a session goes badly — don't extend it. Stop, break, try next time.
- If a session goes brilliantly — stop on the win. Don't push further.
- If you miss a day — don't double up. Just shift forward.
- 15-20 minutes MAX. Kitchen timer. When it dings, stop.
- The night before is NOT for cramming. It's for confidence.
