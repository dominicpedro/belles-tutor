## Page 39 — Transformations: Translation, Reflection & Rotation (Space & Shape)

### Concepts
- **Translation** (sliding): moving a shape from one position to another without turning or flipping it
- **Reflection** (flipping): flipping a shape over a line to create its mirror image
- **Rotation** (turning): turning a shape around a fixed point
- Recognising and extending geometric patterns that use translations, reflections and rotations of arrow shapes
- Identifying which transformation was used in a pattern
- Comparing two patterns to identify what is the same and what is different about the transformations applied

### Key Rules
- **Translation**: When we slide a shape without turning it, we say the shape has been **translated**.
- **Reflection**: When a shape is flipped over to create its mirror image, we say that the shape has been **reflected**.
- **Rotation**: When a shape is turned around a point, we say the shape has been **rotated**.
- A pattern can combine more than one type of transformation (e.g., a shape may be both reflected and translated in the same sequence).
- The position of the shape changes in a translation; the orientation (which way it faces) changes in a reflection; and the angle changes in a rotation.

### Areas to Focus On
- **Know the three transformation types by name**: Be able to define translation, reflection, and rotation in your own words and recognise each one when you see it
- **Use precise mathematical language**: Instead of saying "I moved it," say "I translated it to the right" or "I reflected it along a vertical line" -- the exam expects correct terminology
- **Distinguish reflection from rotation**: A reflection creates a mirror image (the shape flips); a rotation turns the shape but does not flip it. Practice telling these apart by checking whether the shape is a mirror image or simply turned
- **Hands-on practice**: Cut out arrow shapes and physically slide, flip, and turn them on dot paper -- this builds strong intuition for how each transformation works
- **Describing patterns**: Practice writing full sentences that explain *which* transformation was used and *how* it was applied (e.g., "The arrow was reflected horizontally so it now points left instead of right")
- **Comparing transformations**: When asked "what is the same and what is different," always address both parts and use the correct vocabulary (translated, reflected, rotated)
