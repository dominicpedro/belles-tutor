## Page 24 тАФ Fractions of a Metre and Kilometre / Decimals on a Number Line (Number & Pattern)

### Concepts
- Finding unit fractions and non-unit fractions of a metre (converting to centimetres)
- Finding unit fractions and non-unit fractions of a kilometre (converting to metres)
- Using the relationships 1 m = 100 cm and 1 km = 1000 m
- Placing fractions (tenths) on a number line from 0 to 1
- Reading values from a number line marked with arrows (decimals between 0 and 2)
- Adding decimals in sequential chains (repeated addition of 0.1 and 0.2)

### Key Rules
- 1 m = 100 cm
- 1 km = 1000 m
- To find a fraction of a metre: divide 100 cm by the denominator, then multiply by the numerator
  - 1/10 of a m = 10 cm
  - 1/5 of a m = 20 cm
- To find a fraction of a kilometre: divide 1000 m by the denominator, then multiply by the numerator
  - 1/10 of a km = 100 m
  - 1/5 of a km = 200 m
- Tenths on a number line: each interval of 1/10 = 0.1
- When counting in decimals, keep track carefully: adding 0.1 or 0.2 repeatedly builds a decimal sequence

### Areas to Focus On
- Memorise the key conversion facts: 1 m = 100 cm and 1 km = 1000 m
- Practice finding tenths and fifths of 100 and 1000 quickly тАФ these are common in Grade 5 assessments
- Recognise equivalent fractions on a number line: 1/5 = 2/10, 2/5 = 4/10, 3/5 = 6/10, 4/5 = 8/10
- When placing fractions on a number line, first convert fifths to tenths so you can use the same scale
- For decimal addition chains, work carefully step by step тАФ write down each intermediate result to avoid accumulation errors
- Check whether a chain is adding or subtracting (some chains decrease); look at the pattern direction
- Double-check Question 1c: 3/10 of 100 cm = 30 cm (not 20 cm) тАФ this is a common slip when confusing tenths with fifths
- Practice reading unmarked positions on a number line by first identifying the scale (what each small interval represents)
