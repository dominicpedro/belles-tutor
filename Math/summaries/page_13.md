## Page 13 — Rounding and Compensating Strategy for Addition (Number & Pattern)

### Concepts
- Using rounding and compensating as a mental calculation strategy for addition
- Adding two 3-digit numbers by rounding one number to a convenient value, then compensating (adjusting) the answer
- Word problems involving addition of distances (kilometres)
- Selecting pairs of numbers that add up to approximately 800
- Determining different combinations of numbers with a given sum

### Key Rules
- **Rounding and Compensating Strategy**: Round one of the numbers to the nearest ten or hundred to make the addition easier, then adjust (compensate) by adding or subtracting the difference.
  - Example from the page (Dan's method): 58 + 84 is the same as 60 + 82, which is 142 km.
  - Example from the page (Adila's method): I do it differently — 59 + 84 is 59 + 84 = 2, which is 144 − 2 = 142 km.
- Both methods arrive at the same answer by rounding in different ways and then compensating.

### Questions & Answers

**Question 1** (Word Problem — Dan and Adila's box):
> The Magoo family drove 74 km on a tarred road and then 39 km on a gravel road to get to their camp site. How many kilometres did they drive?

**Answer:** 74 + 39 = 113 km

### Areas to Focus On
- **Practise the rounding and compensating method**: Round one number UP to the nearest ten, and subtract that same amount from the other number (or from the result). For example, 57 + 39: round 39 up to 40, so calculate 57 + 40 = 97, then compensate by subtracting 1 to get 96.
- **Compensating in the other direction**: You can also round DOWN and then add the difference. Both approaches must give the same answer.
- **Show your working**: The page specifically asks to "show your thinking" — always write out the rounding step and the compensating step.
- **Word problems**: Read carefully to identify which numbers to add and what the question is actually asking (total distance, difference, etc.).
- **Estimation and number sense**: Question 5 tests the ability to estimate sums and find combinations — practise scanning numbers and mentally estimating whether their sum is close to a target.
- **Systematic combination finding**: For Question 5(b), work through pairs and triples methodically rather than guessing randomly.
