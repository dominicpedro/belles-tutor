## Page 14 — Rounding and Compensating Strategy for Addition & Word Problems (Number & Operations)

### Concepts
- Using the "rounding and compensating" strategy to add decimal numbers (rands and cents, kilometres)
- Rounding one number to a whole or simpler number, then compensating (adding back or subtracting) the difference
- Adding distances (kilometres) with decimals
- Money word problems involving addition of rands and cents
- Multi-step word problems (total cost, change/difference)

### Key Rules
- **Rounding and compensating strategy**: Round one addend to a nearby "friendly" number (usually a whole number or a multiple of 10), perform the addition, then compensate by adjusting for what you added or subtracted during rounding.
  - Example: 27.4 + 2 = 29.4; then compensate: 29.4 - 0.2 = 29.2 km
- When adding money amounts, the same strategy applies: round one price to a simpler value, add, then adjust.

### Areas to Focus On
- **Understand the strategy thoroughly**: The key idea is to round one (or both) numbers to make addition easier, then compensate (subtract what was added, or add what was subtracted) to get the exact answer.
- **Decimal precision**: Be very careful with the compensation amount — track exactly how much you rounded by (e.g., rounding 5.99 up by 0.01, or 2.98 up by 0.02).
- **Money problems require compensating for BOTH amounts**: When both prices are rounded up, you must subtract both compensation amounts at the end.
- **Practice estimating first**: Before calculating, estimate the answer to check if your final result is reasonable.
- **Watch the units**: Some problems use kilometres (km), others use rands and cents (R). Always include the correct unit in the answer.
- **Common mistake**: Forgetting to compensate, or compensating in the wrong direction (adding instead of subtracting, or vice versa).
