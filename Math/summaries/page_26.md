## Page 26 — Adding by Making Pairs (Number & Pattern)

### Concepts
- Adding two amounts of money (Rands) using mental strategies
- Breaking numbers into convenient pairs to make addition easier
- Pairing parts of numbers to create round/friendly numbers (multiples of 10 or 100)
- Solving word problems involving saving and earning money
- Showing your thinking / working when calculating

### Key Rules
- **Pairing strategy**: Break each number into parts that combine to make friendly numbers (multiples of 10 or 100), then add the remaining parts.
  - Example: 280 + 126 --> Think of it as 280 + 120 = 400, and 400 + 6 = R406
  - Example: 168 + 150 --> Think of it as 150 + 150 = 300, and 300 + 18 = R318
- When adding two numbers, look for parts that make 100, 200, or other round numbers.
- Always show your pairing/thinking to demonstrate your method.

### Areas to Focus On
- **Understand the pairing strategy**: The key skill is breaking one of the numbers into parts that combine with the other number to make a round number (e.g., 75 + 27 becomes 75 + 25 + 2, because 75 + 25 = 100).
- **Practice identifying friendly pairs**: Look for parts that make 100, 200, 300, etc. For example, if one number ends in 65, pair it with 35 from the other number.
- **Check arithmetic carefully**: Several handwritten answers in the workbook appear to have small calculation errors. Always double-check by adding the original numbers a second way.
- **Show your working**: The exercises specifically ask "Show your thinking" -- practice writing out the pairing steps (e.g., "280 + 120 = 400, then 400 + 6 = 406").
- **Word problems**: Read carefully to identify the two amounts to add. The context is always about money (savings + earnings), so the answer should be in Rands (R).
- **Carrying over**: When the ones digits add to more than 10, or the tens digits add to more than 100, be careful to carry correctly -- this is where most mistakes happen.
