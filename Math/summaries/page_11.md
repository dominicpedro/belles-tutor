## Page 11 — Adding Decimal Numbers (Number & Pattern)

### Concepts
- Adding decimal numbers (tenths) using mental math strategies
- "Breaking up" strategy: splitting numbers into whole parts and decimal parts, then adding separately
- "Adding from left to right" strategy: adding digits column by column from left to right
- Solving word problems involving addition of decimals (kilometres)
- Completing addition chains (flow diagrams) with decimals

### Key Rules
- **Breaking up strategy**: Split each decimal number into its whole number and decimal fraction, add the whole numbers together, add the decimal parts together, then combine the two results.
  - Example: 8.7 + 5.5 — break into (8 + 5) and (0.7 + 0.5). That is 13 + 1.2 = 14.2
- **Adding from left to right**: Start with the largest place value and work to the right, accumulating the total step by step.
  - Example: 8.7 + 5.5 — start with 8 + 5 = 13, then 0.7 + 0.5 = 1.2, so 13 + 1.2 = 14.2
- When adding decimals, line up the decimal points (tenths to tenths, ones to ones, etc.)

### Areas to Focus On
- Practice breaking decimal numbers apart into whole numbers and decimal fractions before adding — this makes mental math much easier
- Make sure to regroup when decimal parts add up to more than 1.0 (e.g., 0.7 + 0.5 = 1.2, carry the 1 to the whole number)
- Pay close attention to word problems: identify the operation (addition) and include the unit (km) in the answer
- Work through addition chains carefully — each step builds on the previous answer, so one mistake will affect all following answers
- Double-check by estimating: round each decimal to the nearest whole number to see if your answer is reasonable (e.g., 8.7 + 5.5 is roughly 9 + 6 = 15, so 14.2 is reasonable)
- Practice the "adding from left to right" method as it builds number sense for working with decimals mentally
