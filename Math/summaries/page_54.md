## Page 54 — Bar Graphs, Frequency Tables & Mode (Data Handling)

### Concepts
- Reading and interpreting tally marks to determine frequency
- Completing a frequency table from tally data
- Drawing and completing a bar graph from frequency data
- Adding values (scale) to the vertical axis of a bar graph
- Understanding the **mode** as the value/category that occurs most often
- Summarising data using frequency tables and bar graphs
- Distinguishing what the mode does and does not tell us about a data set

### Key Rules
- **Tally marks**: groups of 5 are written as four vertical lines crossed by a diagonal (||||). Count each group of 5 plus any remaining strokes to get the frequency.
- **Frequency**: the number of times each category/value appears in the data.
- **Mode**: the value (number) or category (e.g. shirt colour) that occurs **most often** in a data set. It is the most popular/frequent item.
- The mode tells us which category is most popular, but it does **not** tell us about the colour (or nature) of every item in the data set — only the most common one.
- When drawing a bar graph, ensure:
  - Each bar represents one category.
  - The vertical axis is labelled with evenly spaced numbers.
  - Bars are the correct height to match the frequency.

**Information Box (key definition):**
> It is often useful to summarise data in a way that avoids us having to refer to the frequency table or graph of the data. One way of doing so is to consider the value (number) or category (e.g. shirt colour) that occurs the most often. We call the value (number) or category (e.g. shirt colour) that occurs most often the **mode** of the data.

---

**1e.** Study the bar graph of Bulani's data. What colour is the mode?
> **Answer:** **Black** is the mode (the most popular colour, with a frequency of 15).

**1f.** What does the mode tell us about the colour of the shirts that Bulani sells?
> **Answer:** Which one is the **most popular**. (The mode identifies the colour sold most frequently.)

### Areas to Focus On
- **Counting tally marks carefully**: Pay close attention to groups of 5. A common mistake is miscounting tallies, especially when there are multiple groups.
- **Scaling the vertical axis correctly**: The axis must go high enough to accommodate the largest frequency (here, 15) and use even, consistent intervals (e.g. counting by 2s).
- **Drawing bars accurately**: Each bar must reach exactly the correct height. Use a ruler and check against the scale.
- **Understanding mode vs. other measures**: The mode is only about what occurs most often. It does not tell you the average, the total, or anything about less common values.
- **Interpreting what the mode does NOT reveal**: This is a higher-order thinking question (marked with *). Practise explaining the limitations of statistical measures, not just what they show.
- **Reading bar graphs**: Be comfortable reading values off bar graphs in both directions -- given a bar, state the frequency; given a frequency, identify the category.
