## Page 9 — Sharing (Equal Division Among Friends) (Number & Pattern)

### Concepts
- Equal sharing (division) of a cost among a group of people
- Relationship between the number of people sharing and the cost per person
- Recognising that as the number of sharers increases, the cost per person decreases
- Recognising that when the number of sharers doubles, the cost per person halves
- Completing a table to show total cost, number of rides, and cost per ride
- Multiplication and division as inverse operations

### Key Rules
- **Cost per person = Total cost / Number of people sharing**
- When the number of people sharing **doubles**, the cost per person **halves**
- When the number of people sharing **increases**, the cost per person **decreases** (inverse relationship)
- To find the cost per person, divide the total amount equally among all the people

**Question 2:**
Some friends hire a minibus for R480. The total cost is shared equally among the friends using the table.

**a. Complete the cost sharing table:**

| Number of people sharing | 1   | 3   | 6   | 8   | 10  | 12  |
|--------------------------|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| Cost per person (R)      | 480 | 160 | **80** | **60** | **48** | **40** |

**Correct answers:**
- 1 person: R480 each (480 / 1 = 480)
- 3 people: R160 each (480 / 3 = 160)
- 6 people: R80 each (480 / 6 = 80)
- 8 people: R60 each (480 / 8 = 60)
- 10 people: R48 each (480 / 10 = 48)
- 12 people: R40 each (480 / 12 = 40)

**b. Write in words how you calculated the cost per person.**
**Answer:** I divided R480 by the number of people sharing.
*(Student wrote: "I divided R480 by the number of people.")*

**c. What do you notice when you multiply the number of people sharing (input number) by the cost per person (output number)?**
**Answer:** You get R480 each time. (The product is always R480 — the total cost stays the same.)

**d. What happens to the cost per person if the number of people doubles?**
- From 1 to 2: The cost per person is halved
- From 2 to 4: It halved
- From 5 to 10: It halved
- From 6 to 12: It halved

**Answer:** When the number of people doubles, the cost per person halves.

**e. What happens to the cost per person if the number of people changes:**
- From 1 to 3? **You get a third** (cost goes from R480 to R160; when you divide by 3, you get R160)
- From 3 to 6? **It halves** (cost goes from R160 to R80)
- From 3 to 9? **You get a third of R480 = R160, then a third of that... i.e. it is divided by 3 again**

*(Student's written answers: "when you divide you get to 3", and similar reasoning.)*

**f. Describe any patterns you may have noticed.**
**Answer:** The more people that share, the lower the cost gets. With the more people you get, the lower the cost got.
*(Student wrote: "The more people you get sharing, with the lower the cost got.")*

### Areas to Focus On
- Practice dividing larger numbers (like 480) by single-digit and two-digit divisors mentally and on paper
- Understand the **inverse relationship**: more people sharing = lower cost per person
- Know that **doubling the number of sharers halves the cost** — this is a key pattern to recognise
- Be able to explain the relationship in words (the product of the number of people and cost per person always equals the total)
- Practice completing tables by identifying the rule/pattern first, then applying it consistently
- Check answers by multiplying: number of people x cost per person should always equal the total (R480)
- Recognise that this is essentially division: Total / Number of people = Cost per person
