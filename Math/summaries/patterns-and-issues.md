## Common Mistakes

### 1. Rounding Larger Numbers (4-digit numbers)
**Pages:** 1
**Details:**
- Rounded 2389 to nearest 5: wrote 2400 instead of 2390
- Rounded 2389 to nearest 10: wrote 2400 instead of 2390
- Rounded 2389 to nearest 100: wrote 2000 instead of 2400
- Rounded 3976 to nearest 10: wrote 4000 instead of 3980
**Pattern:** When numbers exceed ~2000, Belle confuses which digit determines the rounding. She appears to jump to the nearest thousand instead of applying the correct rounding level. The error is worse when the same number must be rounded to multiple levels (nearest 5, 10, AND 100).

### 2. Decimal Counting Across Whole-Number Boundaries
**Pages:** 2
**Details:**
- In flow diagram (b), wrote 0,1 repeatedly instead of 1,1, 1,2, 1,3, 1,4
- Wrote 0,6 instead of 0,8 and 0,16 instead of 1,0 in the counting chain
**Pattern:** When adding 0,1 repeatedly past 0,9, Belle does not transition to 1,0. She either restarts at 0,1 or produces values like 0,16 (concatenating digits instead of carrying). This indicates a conceptual gap in understanding that 0,9 + 0,1 = 1,0 (the ones digit increases).

### 3. Ratio/Proportion with Non-Table Values
**Pages:** 3
**Details:**
- Q1b: For 80 litres of blue paint (ratio 4:3), wrote 19 litres of green instead of 60
- Q1c: For 90 litres of green paint, wrote 89 litres of blue instead of 120
- Q1d: For 1 litre of blue paint, wrote 99 litres of green instead of 0,75
**Pattern:** Belle can complete ratio tables by adding/multiplying sequentially (she got the table in Q1a correct), but she cannot apply the ratio relationship to values outside the table. She does not use the multiplicative relationship (green = blue x 3/4) and instead appears to guess or use incorrect operations. This is a significant conceptual gap -- she can follow a pattern but cannot reason proportionally.

### 4. Cost Table Errors for Large Quantities
**Pages:** 3, 7
**Details:**
- Page 3 Q2c: 54 packets at R6 each -- wrote R138 instead of R324 (should be R300 + R24)
- Page 7 Q1c: 400 soccer balls at R56 each -- student working shows R346 + R174 + R314 (incorrect decomposition)
- Page 7 Q1d: 430 balls -- wrote R21 060 + R2 360 instead of R22 400 + R1 680
- Page 7 Q1e: 438 balls -- wrote R13 900 + R438 + R1 238 instead of R22 400 + R1 680 + R448
**Pattern:** Belle struggles when cost table problems require extending beyond the given table or working with hundreds. She can use the decomposition strategy for smaller quantities (e.g., 9 = 5 + 4, 23 = 20 + 3, 28 = 20 + 8) but breaks down when scaling to hundreds. The arithmetic errors suggest she may not be reading the table values correctly for larger multipliers.

### 5. Fractions of Amounts (Division Errors)
**Pages:** 7
**Details:**
- Q2c: 1/10 of R50 -- wrote R2.50 instead of R5
- Q2d: 1/10 of R15 -- wrote R0.75 instead of R1.50
**Pattern:** Belle understands that "1/10 of" means dividing, but makes arithmetic errors when the division result is not a whole number or when the amount is less familiar. For R20 and R40 (whole-number results) she was correct; for R50 and R15 she was not.

### 6. Counting Total Parts in Fraction-of-Shape Problems
**Pages:** 18
**Details:**
- Q1e: Cross/plus shape made of 5 equal squares, 3 shaded -- wrote 3/7 instead of 3/5
**Pattern:** Belle miscounted the total number of equal parts in a non-rectangular shape. She may have counted extra "spaces" or misidentified the denominator. This error is specific to irregular or composite shapes rather than simple rectangles/circles.

**Pattern:** When adding multiple unit fractions of an hour, Belle may skip a term or lose track of the first conversion. Her working shows "10 + 12 = 22" suggesting she forgot to include the 30 minutes from 1/2.

### 9. Division-Based Word Problems (Dividing Large Amounts)
**Pages:** 30
**Details:**
- Q5: Lara saves R150/month for 7 months -- wrote R1 500 instead of R1 050 (multiplied by 10 instead of 7)
- Q6a: R10 000 / R1 500 per ticket -- wrote 18 instead of 6 (with R1 000 left over)
- Q6b: R12 000 / R1 500 per ticket -- wrote 20 instead of 8
- Q6c: R150 000 / R1 500 per ticket -- wrote 22 instead of 100
- Q2c: 5th multiple of 150 -- wrote 756 instead of 750 (arithmetic slip)
**Pattern:** Belle has serious difficulty with division of larger numbers, particularly when dividing by R1 500. She does not appear to use the relationship between the multiples she counted in Q1 (counting in 1 500s) and the division questions in Q6. Her answers (18, 20, 22) suggest she may be counting or guessing rather than dividing. She also confused multiplication by 7 vs 10 in the savings problem. This is a critical gap: she cannot connect multiples/skip-counting to division word problems.

### 10. 24-Hour Time Conversion Errors
**Pages:** 49
**Details:**
- Q2d: "Half past 10 at night" -- wrote 9:45 pm / 21:45 instead of 10:30 pm / 22:30 (confused "half past" with "quarter to")
- Q2g: "20 minutes past 7 in the morning" -- wrote 01:20 in 24-hour time instead of 07:20 (forgot the leading zero or confused 7 with 1)
- Q2h: "20 minutes past 1 in the afternoon" -- wrote 8:20 pm instead of 1:20 pm / 13:20
- Q2i: "A quarter to 9 in the evening" -- wrote 08:45 instead of 20:45 (forgot to add 12 for PM)
**Pattern:** Belle makes two recurring types of time errors: (1) she confuses time phrases like "half past" and "quarter to," and (2) she forgets to add 12 when converting PM times to 24-hour format. The "half past" confusion (writing 9:45 instead of 10:30) is particularly concerning as it suggests the phrase meaning is not fully internalised.

### 11. Time Interval / Elapsed Time Errors
**Pages:** 51, 52
**Details:**
- Page 51 Q5: Adding 45 minutes repeatedly from 11:00 -- wrote 12:25 instead of 12:30 for the second visit (error when crossing the hour boundary at 11:45 + 0:45)
- Page 51 Q5: Subsequent visits also wrong (1:16 instead of 1:15, 1:55 instead of 2:00)
- Page 52 Q1a: 10:24 to 11:00 -- wrote 37 min instead of 36 min
- Page 52 Q1b: 10:24 to 11:20 -- wrote 50 min instead of 56 min
- Page 52 Q1d: 11:20 to 14:00 -- wrote 4 hours instead of 2 h 40 min
- Page 52 Q1e: 7:45 am to 2:35 pm -- wrote 2 hours instead of 6 h 50 min (failed to account for am/pm transition)
- Page 52 Q1f: 21:00 to 08:30 -- wrote 5 h 30 min instead of 11 h 30 min (failed to account for crossing midnight)
- Page 52 Q1i: 16:15:35 to 16:16:10 -- wrote 1 min 25 sec instead of 35 seconds
- Page 52 Q1j: 16:50:20 to 17:10:35 -- wrote 1 h 10 min 15 sec instead of 20 min 15 sec
**Pattern:** Belle struggles significantly with elapsed time calculations, especially when they cross hour boundaries, am/pm boundaries, or midnight. She cannot reliably count forward from one time to another. When times span am to pm (e.g., 7:45 am to 2:35 pm), she undercounts drastically (writing 2 hours instead of 6 h 50 min). Crossing midnight is also a major weakness (writing 5 h 30 min instead of 11 h 30 min). This is the highest error density of any single page.

### 12. Time Subtraction / Borrowing Errors
**Pages:** 52, 53
**Details:**
- Page 52 Q2: 3 min 10 sec minus 2 min 45 sec -- wrote 1 min 35 sec instead of 25 seconds (subtracted 10 from 45 instead of borrowing: 2 min 70 sec - 2 min 45 sec = 25 sec)
- Page 52 Q3: 01:30:48 to 01:31:25 -- wrote 1 min 23 sec instead of 37 seconds (did not handle borrowing from minutes to seconds)
- Page 53 Q1a: Chicken in oven at 4:30 pm for 1 h 20 min -- wrote 5:20 pm instead of 5:50 pm (added only 50 minutes or miscalculated)
- Page 53 Q3: Show ending at 23:00, total duration 2 h 50 min -- wrote 21:50 instead of 20:10 (subtracted only 1 h 10 min instead of 2 h 50 min)
- Page 53 Q5: 2:40 pm to 5:25 pm -- wrote 3 h 15 min instead of 2 h 45 min (added instead of finding the correct elapsed time)
**Pattern:** Belle does not understand borrowing in time subtraction. When seconds or minutes in the subtrahend are larger than in the minuend, she either reverses the subtraction (45 - 10 instead of borrowing to get 70 - 45) or produces incorrect results. She also struggles with backward time calculations (finding start times by subtracting duration). Multi-step time problems (combining duration + interval before subtracting) are especially error-prone.

### 13. Arithmetic Errors in Addition Pairing Problems
**Pages:** 26
**Details:**
- Q3a: 70 + 86 -- wrote 146 instead of 156
- Q3c: 120 + 166 -- wrote 284 instead of 286
- Q3f: 250 + 314 -- wrote 544 instead of 564
- Q3g: 230 + 327 -- wrote 5571 instead of 557
**Pattern:** When using the pairing strategy with larger numbers (3-digit addition), Belle makes carrying errors. The pairing approach is understood conceptually but the final arithmetic step sometimes goes wrong, particularly when combining the paired total with the remaining part.

### 14. Weak Transformation Vocabulary
**Pages:** 39
**Details:**
- Q2: Asked "What is the same and what is different about how you moved the shape for the last two patterns?" -- Belle wrote "You just put it into another position" instead of using the correct terms (reflection, rotation, translation)
**Pattern:** Belle can physically perform transformations (her drawings received tick marks and "YAY" from the teacher), but she does not use the correct mathematical vocabulary when describing what she did. She needs to consistently use the terms "translated" (slid), "reflected" (flipped), and "rotated" (turned) in written explanations.

### Working Style (observed Days 6-7)

- **All working done in her head** — never writes steps down unless prompted
- **Non-linear thinking** — jumps between start, end, and middle of a problem rather than working sequentially
- **Impact:** Simple problems fit in working memory and come out correct; complex problems (3+ steps) overload working memory and produce errors
- **Mitigation:** 4 named methods ("superpowers") with written and typed shorthand formats that force step-by-step working (see `notes/method_superpowers-summary.md`)
- **Day 7 update:** Belle now writes working unprompted for Bunny Hops, Double Up, and PM = Plus. Round & Fix working is written but fix-step arithmetic slipped (525-3 = 523 initially, self-corrected to 522 with prompting). Method selection (knowing which method to use) is emerging but not automatic — needed a prompt to distinguish PM=Plus from Bunny Hops on a "how long" question.
