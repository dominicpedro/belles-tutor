## Page 10 — Problem Solving (Number & Pattern / Financial Mathematics)

### Concepts
- Solving multi-step word problems involving money
- Subtraction with large numbers (thousands)
- Multiplication to calculate totals (cost and income)
- Completing number pattern tables (finding fractions of numbers)
- Understanding unit cost and total revenue
- Working with fractions of whole numbers (1/2, 1/3, 1/6 of a number)

### Key Rules
- To find how much more is needed: Total needed - Amount already have = Amount still needed
- To find total cost: Number of items x Cost per item = Total cost
- To find total income: Number of tickets x Price per ticket = Total income
- Fractions of a number: divide the number by the denominator
  - 1/2 of a number = number / 2
  - 1/3 of a number = number / 3
  - 1/6 of a number = number / 6

### Questions & Answers

**Question 1.**
The school needs R7 000 to build a new wall around the school. They already have R3 580.

**(a)** How much money do they still need?

- Vusi solved the problem like this:
  - 7 000 - 3 580
  - I start by counting on
  - 3 580 + 20 = 3 600
  - 3 600 + 400 = 4 000
  - and 4 000 + 3 000 = 7 000
  - They need 20 + 400 + 3 000 = **R3 420**

**(b)** How did Vusi solve this?

- Vusi solved it by counting on from R3 580 up to R7 000, adding amounts step by step, then adding all the jumps together.
- **Answer:** Vusi used the **"adding on" method** (also called counting on or building up to the total).

**Granny's method:**
- 7 000 - 3 580 = 3 420 using vertical subtraction method
- Granny calculated: 7000 - 3580 = **R3 420**

**Question 2.**
A netball ball costs R120. How many balls can the school buy if they have R500?

- Calculation: 500 / 120 = 4 remainder 20
- **Answer:** The school can buy **4 balls** (4 x 120 = R480, with R20 left over, which is not enough for another ball)

**Question 3.**
A ticket for the school concert costs R5. How much money will the school get if:

**(a)** 50 people each buy a ticket?
- 50 x R5 = **R250**

**(b)** 300 people each buy a ticket?
- 300 x R5 = **R1 500**

**(c)** 350 people each buy a ticket?
- 350 x R5 = **R1 750**

**(d)** 650 people each buy a ticket?
- 650 x R5 = **R3 250**

Student working shown: 50 x 5 = R250; 300 x 5 = R1 500; 350 x 5 = R1 750; 650 x 5 = R3 250

### Areas to Focus On
- **Subtraction strategies:** Practice both "counting on" (building up) and standard vertical subtraction for large numbers. Understand why both methods give the same answer.
- **Division with remainders:** When a word problem asks "how many can you buy," remember to round DOWN because you cannot buy part of an item. Always check if the remainder is enough for one more.
- **Multiplication with money:** Be comfortable multiplying by 5, 10, 50, 100, and 300. Break larger multiplications into parts (e.g., 350 x 5 = 300 x 5 + 50 x 5).
- **Fractions of whole numbers:** Remember that finding 1/6 means dividing by 6, finding 1/2 means dividing by 2, and finding 1/3 means dividing by 3. Notice the relationship: 1/6 of a number is always half of 1/3 of that number.
- **Reading word problems carefully:** Identify what operation is needed (addition, subtraction, multiplication, or division) from the context of the problem.
- **Checking your work:** Use the inverse operation to verify answers (e.g., if 7000 - 3580 = 3420, check that 3580 + 3420 = 7000).
