## Page 19 — Fractions of an Hour / Time and Calendars (Number & Pattern)

### Concepts
- Converting between hours and minutes using a table
- Understanding fractions of an hour (1/2, 1/4, 1/5, 1/6 of 60 minutes)
- Writing different fractions of an hour that equal a given number of minutes
- Adding unit fractions of an hour to find a total in minutes
- Counting the number of days in specific months of the year
- Calculating a person's age from their birth year

### Key Rules
- 1 hour = 60 minutes
- 1/2 of an hour = 30 minutes
- 1/4 of an hour = 15 minutes
- 1/5 of an hour = 12 minutes
- 1/6 of an hour = 10 minutes
- To find a fraction of an hour: divide 60 by the denominator
- Days in each month: January (31), February (28 or 29), March (31), April (30), May (31), June (30), July (31), August (31), September (30), October (31), November (30), December (31)
- Days from January to June: 31 + 28 + 31 + 30 + 31 + 30 = 181 days (non-leap year)
- Days from July to December: 31 + 31 + 30 + 31 + 30 + 31 = 184 days

### Areas to Focus On
- Memorise the key fractions of an hour: 1/2 = 30 min, 1/4 = 15 min, 1/5 = 12 min, 1/6 = 10 min
- Practice converting between fraction-of-an-hour notation and minutes quickly
- When adding fractions of an hour, convert each fraction to minutes first, then add the minutes together
- Know the number of days in each month (use the knuckle trick: knuckle months = 31 days, valleys = 30 days, except February = 28/29)
- For age calculations, subtract birth year from current year; check whether the birthday has passed yet for exact age
- Equivalent fractions: recognise that 1/2 = 2/4 = 3/6 (and similar) when working with fractions of time
- Double-check addition carefully — small arithmetic errors are common when adding multiple month-day totals
