## Page 51 — Measurement: Time (Measurement)

### Concepts
- Converting between minutes and seconds (1 minute = 60 seconds)
- Converting between hours and minutes (1 hour = 60 minutes)
- Working with fractions of a minute expressed as seconds
- Understanding elapsed time and time calculations
- Reading and calculating race/finishing times
- Adding durations of time (minutes and seconds)
- Calculating future times from a starting time at regular intervals

### Key Rules
- 1 minute = 60 seconds
- 1 hour = 60 minutes
- To convert a fraction of a minute to seconds, multiply the fraction by 60
  - e.g. 1/10 minute = 60 / 10 = 6 seconds
- To find how much time is under/over a goal, subtract the actual time from the goal time (or vice versa)
- 1.5 hours = 1 hour and 30 minutes (not 1 hour and 50 minutes)

**Question 4:**

The lengths of the 3 songs on a playlist are given as 4:48, 4:32 and 3:56. How much time is that altogether?

**Answer:**
- 4:48 + 4:32 + 3:56
- Add seconds: 48 + 32 + 56 = 136 seconds = 2 minutes 16 seconds
- Add minutes: 4 + 4 + 3 = 11 minutes
- Total: 11 minutes + 2 minutes 16 seconds = **13 minutes and 16 seconds**

**Question 5:**

A nurse checks her patient in the hospital every forty-five minutes. She starts at 11 o'clock in the morning. Write down the times of the next 4 visits.

**Answer:**
- Visit 1: **11:45 am**

Working: Starting at 11:00 am, add 45 minutes each time:

### Areas to Focus On
- **Fraction-to-seconds conversion:** Practice converting fractions and decimals of a minute into seconds by multiplying by 60. This is a foundational skill tested heavily in Question 1.
- **Decimal vs. fraction confusion with time:** Understand that 1.5 hours means 1 and a HALF hour (30 minutes), not 1 hour 50 minutes. The decimal point in time does not work the same as in regular base-10 numbers because time uses base-60.
- **Subtracting times:** Practice subtracting hours, minutes, and seconds carefully, borrowing where needed (e.g., borrowing 1 minute = 60 seconds). Question 3 requires precise subtraction.
- **Adding durations with carrying:** When seconds add up to more than 60, convert to minutes. When minutes add up to more than 60, convert to hours (Question 4).
- **Repeated addition of time intervals:** Question 5 requires adding 45 minutes repeatedly. Be careful when crossing the hour mark (e.g., 11:45 + 45 minutes crosses into 12:30, not 12:25). Watch out for errors when adding minutes that cross the 60-minute boundary.
- **Student errors noted:** The student's answers to Question 5 appear to contain errors (12:25 instead of 12:30, etc.), suggesting difficulty with carrying over minutes past the hour. Extra practice with elapsed time problems crossing the hour is recommended.
