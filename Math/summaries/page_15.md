## Page 15 — Ratio Tables & Decimal Fractions (Number & Patterns)

### Concepts
- Using ratio tables to determine cost based on a constant rate (proportional reasoning)
- Multiplying to scale up values in a ratio table
- Understanding decimal fractions (tenths) and their relationship to common fractions
- Writing number names using decimal notation (e.g., 2,3 means 2 and 3 tenths)
- Adding fractions with denominator 10 and converting to decimal form

### Key Rules
- **Ratio tables**: If 1 blanket costs a certain amount, multiply the cost by the number of blankets to find the total. The relationship between columns must stay proportional.
- **Decimal fractions (tenths)**:
  - 1/10 = 0,1
  - 2/10 = 0,2
  - The digit after the decimal comma represents tenths.
  - A number like 2,3 means 2 + 3/10 (2 whole and 3 tenths).
- When adding a whole number and tenths: write the whole number, then a comma, then the tenths digit.

### Questions & Answers

#### Question 1
**Thembah's factory sells blankets at R73 each. Complete the table.**

| Number of blankets | 1   | 2   | 3   | 4   | 5   | 6   | 7   | 8   | 9   |
|--------------------|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| Cost (R)           | 73  | 146 | 219 | 292 | 365 | 438 | 511 | 584 | 657 |

| Number of blankets | 10  | 20  | 30  | 40  | 50  | 60  | 70  | 80  | 90  |
|--------------------|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| Cost (R)           | 730 | 1 460 | 2 190 | 2 920 | 3 650 | 4 380 | 5 110 | 5 840 | 6 570 |

| Number of blankets | 100   | 200   | 300   | 400   | 500   | 600   | 700   | 800   | 900   |
|--------------------|-------|-------|-------|-------|-------|-------|-------|-------|-------|
| Cost (R)           | 7 300 | 14 600 | 21 900 | 29 200 | 36 500 | 43 800 | 51 100 | 58 400 | 65 700 |

**Use the tables to determine:**

**(a) What 36 blankets will cost:**
- 30 blankets = R2 190
- 6 blankets = R438
- 36 blankets = R2 190 + R438 = **R2 628**
- Explain: Break 36 into 30 + 6, look up each in the table, and add the costs together.

**(b) What 485 blankets will cost:**
- 400 blankets = R29 200
- 80 blankets = R5 840
- 5 blankets = R365
- 485 blankets = R29 200 + R5 840 + R365 = **R35 405**
- Explain: Break 485 into 400 + 80 + 5, look up each in the table, and add.

**(c) What is 297 x 73?**
- 200 blankets = R14 600
- 90 blankets = R6 570
- 7 blankets = R511
- 297 x 73 = R14 600 + R6 570 + R511 = **R21 681**
- Explain: Break 297 into 200 + 90 + 7, find each cost in the table, and add.

**(d) What is 618 x 73?**
- 600 blankets = R43 800
- 10 blankets = R730
- 8 blankets = R584
- 618 x 73 = R43 800 + R730 + R584 = **R45 114**
- Explain: Break 618 into 600 + 10 + 8, find each cost in the table, and add.

#### Question 2
**In this number chain you will add tenths, but you must write your answer using decimal notation. The first few have been done for you.**

Given information:
- 1/10 = 0,1
- 2/10 = 0,2

**Number chain — each step adds a fraction in tenths to the running total:**

| Step | Calculation | Answer |
|------|------------|--------|
| Start | 2 | 2 |
| +4/10 | 2 + 4/10 | **2,4** |
| +3/10 | 2,4 + 3/10 | **2,7** (i.e., 2 + 7/10) |
| +3/10 | 2,7 + 3/10 | **3,0** (i.e., 3 + 0/10) |
| +1/10 | 3,0 + 1/10 | **3,1** |
| +2/10 | 3,1 + 2/10 | **3,3** |
| +3/10 | 3,3 + 3/10 | **3,6** (i.e., 3 + 6/10) |
| +5/10 | 3,6 + 5/10 | **4,1** |
| +3/10 | 4,1 + 3/10 | **4,4** |
| +1/10 | 4,4 + 1/10 | **4,5** |
| +3/10 | 4,5 + 3/10 | **4,8** |
| +3/10 | 4,8 + 3/10 | **5,1** |
| +2/10 | 5,1 + 2/10 | **5,3** |
| +3/10 | 5,3 + 3/10 | **5,6** (i.e., 5 + 6/10) |
| +1/10 | 5,6 + 1/10 | **5,7** |
| +3/10 | 5,7 + 3/10 | **6,0** |
| +3/10 | 6,0 + 3/10 | **6,3** |
| +5/10 | 6,3 + 5/10 | **6,8** |
| +1/10 | 6,8 + 1/10 | **6,9** (i.e., 6 + 9/10) |

**Note:** South African notation uses a comma (,) as the decimal separator instead of a point (.).

### Areas to Focus On
- **Breaking numbers into place-value parts**: Practise splitting any 3-digit number into hundreds + tens + units to use a ratio table (e.g., 485 = 400 + 80 + 5).
- **Ratio table fluency**: Be comfortable reading values from a table and adding them together. Double-check addition of large numbers carefully.
- **Understanding tenths**: Know that 10/10 = 1 whole, so when tenths add past 9/10, you gain one whole and reset the tenths (e.g., 2,8 + 3/10 = 3,1 not 2,11).
- **Decimal notation**: Remember that the number after the comma is the tenths digit (only one digit for tenths). Practise converting between fraction form (e.g., 3/10) and decimal form (0,3).
- **Carrying over in tenths**: When the tenths add up to 10/10 or more, increase the whole number by 1 and subtract 10 from the tenths (e.g., 6/10 + 5/10 = 11/10 = 1 + 1/10).
- **Careful arithmetic**: In the ratio table question, errors often come from addition mistakes with large numbers. Use estimation to check (e.g., 297 x 73 is roughly 300 x 73 = 21 900, so 21 681 is reasonable).
