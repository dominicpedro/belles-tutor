## Page 12 — Sharing, Fractions & Number Patterns (Number & Pattern)

### Concepts
- Sharing in a given ratio (for every two cookies, Dan gets one)
- Completing tables that show a proportional relationship
- Understanding fractions as equal parts of a whole
- Cutting a shape (pizza) into equal pieces and naming the fraction
- Representing fractions on a diagram
- Number patterns using a constant additive rule (function machines / arrow diagrams)

### Key Rules
- When sharing in the ratio "one for every two," for every 3 cookies total, Mr James gets 2 and Dan gets 1 (2:1 ratio).
- A fraction names equal parts: if a whole is cut into 4 equal pieces, each piece is 1/4.
- If a whole is cut into 2 equal pieces, each piece is 1/2.
- If a whole is cut into 3 equal pieces, each piece is 1/3.
- In an arrow/function diagram, apply the same operation (e.g., + 2.5 or + 1.5) to every input to get the output.

**Question 1**
Mr James buys cookies. He agrees to give Dan one cookie for every two cookies that he keeps for himself.

**(a)** How many cookies will Dan get if they share 36 cookies?
- **Answer:** Dan gets 12 cookies. (Mr James gets 24, Dan gets 12, since the ratio is 2:1.)

**Question 2**
A pizza is cut into 4 equal pieces.

**(a)** What fraction of the pizza is each of the pieces?
- **Answer:** 1/4

**(b)** Each of the pieces is cut into two equal pieces. Show this on the diagram.
- **Answer:** The circle (pizza) should be divided into 8 equal slices (the student draws additional lines through each quarter).

**(c)** What fraction of the pizza is each new piece?
- **Answer:** 1/8

**(a)** (Diagram) — The circle should be divided into 12 equal slices.

**(b)** What fraction of the pizza is each new piece?
- **Answer:** 1/12

### Areas to Focus On
- **Ratio and sharing:** Practice dividing a total into parts using a given ratio (e.g., 2:1). Remember: if the ratio is 2:1, there are 3 parts total. Divide the total by 3 to find one share.
- **Divisibility:** Know which numbers are divisible by 3 (sum of digits divisible by 3) to answer "when can they share without breaking?"
- **Fractions of a whole:** When a shape is cut into equal pieces, the fraction is 1 over the number of pieces (1/n). If each piece is then subdivided, multiply the number of pieces (e.g., 4 pieces each cut into 2 gives 8 pieces, so each is 1/8).
- **Function machines / arrow diagrams:** Identify the rule (e.g., + 2.5) and apply it consistently to every input. Practice adding decimals like 2.5 and 1.5 mentally.
- **Tables:** Look for the pattern in a table (constant difference or ratio) and extend it. Double-check by verifying that Mr James' cookies + Dan's cookies = total number of cookies.
