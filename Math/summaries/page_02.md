### Concepts
- Converting common fractions (tenths) to decimal form
- Understanding that fractions with denominator 10 can be written as decimals
- Placing decimals on a number line
- Counting forwards in steps of 0,1 and 0,2 (decimal increments)
- Solving a money word problem involving decimals/rand amounts

### Key Rules
- 1/10 can also be written as 0,1
- 2/10 can also be written as 0,2
- 27/10 can also be written as 2,7
- 0,1 and 0,2 and 2,7 are called the **decimal form** of the numbers
- To convert a fraction with denominator 10 to a decimal: the numerator's digits go after the decimal comma (e.g. 5/10 = 0,5; 6/10 = 0,6; 82/10 = 8,2)
- In South African notation, a **comma** is used as the decimal separator (e.g. 0,1 not 0.1)

**Solution method (student working visible on page):**

### Areas to Focus On

- **Converting fractions to decimals:** Practice converting tenths (and fifths, which become tenths) to decimal form. Remember 1/5 = 2/10 = 0,2.
- **Number line placement:** Be precise when placing decimals on a number line. Count the intervals carefully and note that the line may extend past 1,0.
- **Counting in decimal steps:** When adding 0,1 repeatedly, remember that 0,9 + 0,1 = 1,0 (not 0,10). The "ones" digit increases when you pass a whole number. Similarly 1,8 + 0,2 = 2,0.
- **Word problems with two unknowns:** Use the strategy: subtract the difference from the total, divide by 2 to find the smaller amount, then add the difference back to find the larger amount. Always check your answer adds back to the total.
- **Watch for careless errors:** Some answers on this page appear to have small mistakes (e.g., writing 0,1 instead of 1,1). Always re-read your answers to check they make sense in the pattern.
- **South African decimal notation:** Remember to use a comma (not a dot) as the decimal separator in South African maths.
