## Page 50 — Conversions: Time (Measurement)

### Concepts
- Converting between units of time (seconds, minutes, hours, days, years, decades, centuries, millenniums)
- Reading and interpreting analogue and digital clocks
- Calculating elapsed time in real-world word problems
- Understanding the hierarchy of time units from smallest (seconds) to largest (millennium)

### Key Rules

**Time Conversion Reference (shown in box on page):**
- 60 seconds = 1 minute
- 60 minutes = 1 hour
- 24 hours = 1 day
- 365 days = 1 year
- 10 years = 1 decade
- 100 years = 1 century
- 1 000 years = 1 millennium

**Question 3: Word Problem — Nadia's Homework**

Nadia spends 40 minutes doing her Mathematics homework and 35 minutes doing her History homework. How long did she spend doing homework?

- **Total time:** 40 + 35 = **75 minutes**
- Converted: **1 hour and 15 minutes**

**Question 4: Word Problem — Dan's Sleep**

Dan sleeps from 22:40 until 08:10.

- a. ___ hours and ___ minutes
  - From 22:40 to 08:10 = **9 hours and 30 minutes** (9 h 30 min)
- b. How many minutes is that?
  - 9 x 60 + 30 = **570 minutes**

**Question 5: Word Problem — Sara's After-School Routine**

When Sara arrives home after school she eats for half an hour, then does homework for three quarters of an hour and helps with chores for 20 minutes. How many minutes is that altogether?

- Eating: 30 minutes (half an hour)
- Homework: 45 minutes (three quarters of an hour)
- Chores: 20 minutes
- **Total: 30 + 45 + 20 = 95 minutes**
- Converted: **1 hour and 35 minutes**

### Areas to Focus On
- **Memorise the conversion table:** Knowing that 60 seconds = 1 minute, 60 minutes = 1 hour, 24 hours = 1 day, 365 days = 1 year is essential for quick mental conversions
- **Converting minutes to hours and minutes:** Practice dividing total minutes by 60 to get hours and using the remainder as leftover minutes (e.g., 200 / 60 = 3 remainder 20, so 3 hours 20 minutes)
- **Elapsed time across midnight:** For problems like Dan's sleep (22:40 to 08:10), count forward from the start time — first to midnight, then from midnight to the end time (22:40 to 00:00 = 1 h 20 min, then 00:00 to 08:10 = 8 h 10 min, total = 9 h 30 min)
- **Word problems with fractions of an hour:** Remember that "half an hour" = 30 minutes and "three quarters of an hour" = 45 minutes
- **Decades, centuries, millenniums:** These larger units are tested less often but appear in Question 2 — remember the pattern of multiplying/dividing by 10 (decade), 100 (century), and 1 000 (millennium)
- **Double-check by converting back:** After solving, convert your answer back to verify (e.g., 9 h 30 min = 570 min, check: 570 / 60 = 9.5 hours = 9 h 30 min)
