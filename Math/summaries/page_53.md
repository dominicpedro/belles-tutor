## Page 53 — Measurement: Time (Measurement)

### Concepts
- Calculating elapsed time (hours and minutes)
- Working with both 12-hour (am/pm) and 24-hour clock formats
- Adding and subtracting time intervals
- Determining start times, end times, and durations
- Solving multi-step time word problems
- Calculating total time over multiple days (weekly totals)

### Key Rules
- **Elapsed time**: End time minus Start time = Duration
- **Finding a start time**: End time minus Duration = Start time
- **Finding an end time**: Start time plus Duration = End time
- **12-hour to 24-hour conversion**: Add 12 to pm hours (e.g., 4:30 pm = 16:30); am hours stay the same (except 12 am = 00:00)
- **Half past** an hour means 30 minutes past (e.g., half past 4 pm = 4:30 pm)
- When calculating intervals that repeat, count how many fit within the total time span
- **Halfway through a duration**: Divide the total duration by 2 and add to the start time (or subtract from the end time)

### Questions & Answers

**1a.** Mrs Brown puts a chicken in the oven at half past 4 pm. It must cook for 1 hour and 20 minutes. At what time must she take it out of the oven?

- Start time: 4:30 pm
- Duration: 1 hour 20 minutes
- 4:30 pm + 1 hour = 5:30 pm; 5:30 pm + 20 minutes = 5:50 pm
- **Answer: 5:50 pm**
- *Student's written answer: 5:20 pm (incorrect -- the student appears to have added only 50 minutes or miscalculated)*

**1b.** Mrs Brown wants dinner to be ready at 7 pm. If a chicken takes 1 hour and 20 minutes to cook, at what time must she put the chicken in the oven so that it will be ready on time?

- End time: 7:00 pm
- Duration: 1 hour 20 minutes
- 7:00 pm - 1 hour = 6:00 pm; 6:00 pm - 20 minutes = 5:40 pm
- **Answer: 5:40 pm**
- *Student's written answer: 05:40 pm (correct)*

**2.** A train stops at a certain station every 35 minutes. The first train stops at the station at 05:00. How many trains will have stopped at the station before 10:00?

- Time span: 05:00 to 10:00 = 5 hours = 300 minutes
- Trains stop every 35 minutes: 05:00, 05:35, 06:10, 06:45, 07:20, 07:55, 08:30, 09:05, 09:40, 10:15 (this one is after 10:00)
- Count trains that stopped before 10:00 (not including the first at 05:00 if we count "will have stopped"): The stops at or before 10:00 are at 05:00, 05:35, 06:10, 06:45, 07:20, 07:55, 08:30, 09:05, 09:40 = 9 stops
- But since the question says "before 10:00", and 09:40 + 35 = 10:15 which is after 10:00, the last stop before 10:00 is 09:40
- Including the 05:00 train: **Answer: 9 trains** (if counting the 05:00 departure) or **8 trains** (if "before 10:00" excludes the first and counts only subsequent stops)
- *Student's written answer: 10 trains*

**3.** A musical show is 2 h 20 min long. Half way through the show there will be an interval of 30 minutes. If the show must end at 23:00, what is the latest time at which it can start?

- Total show time: 2 hours 20 minutes + 30 minutes interval = 2 hours 50 minutes
- End time: 23:00
- 23:00 - 2 hours 50 minutes = 20:10
- **Answer: 20:10 (8:10 pm)**
- *Student's written answer: 21:50 (incorrect -- the student appears to have subtracted only part of the time)*

**4.** Vusi plays Playstation for 40 minutes every day from Monday to Friday. On Saturdays and Sundays he plays for 2 hours per day. How much time does he spend on Playstation during the week?

- Monday to Friday: 5 days x 40 minutes = 200 minutes = 3 hours 20 minutes
- Saturday and Sunday: 2 days x 2 hours = 4 hours = 240 minutes
- Total: 200 + 240 = 440 minutes = 7 hours 20 minutes
- **Answer: 7 hours 20 minutes**
- *Student's written answer: 4 ... hours (partially visible, likely incomplete or incorrect)*

**5.** Dan plays Playstation from 2:40 pm until 5:25 pm. How many hours and minutes did he play for?

- From 2:40 pm to 5:25 pm
- 2:40 to 5:40 = 3 hours; but we need to go to 5:25, which is 15 minutes less
- So: 3 hours - 15 minutes = 2 hours 45 minutes
- **Answer: 2 hours 45 minutes**
- *Student's written answer: 3 hours 15 mins (incorrect -- the student added instead of finding the correct difference)*

### Areas to Focus On
- **Careful direction of calculation**: Know when to add time (finding end time) vs. subtract time (finding start time). Several errors on this page came from mixing these up.
- **Borrowing with time**: When subtracting minutes, if the minutes aren't enough, borrow 1 hour (= 60 minutes) from the hours column -- this is different from normal borrowing (which is by 10).
- **Multi-step time problems**: Question 3 requires combining the show duration AND the interval before subtracting. Practice breaking these into steps: find total time first, then work backward.
- **Counting repeated intervals**: For Question 2 (train stops), physically list out each time rather than just dividing, as it avoids off-by-one errors.
- **Converting between hours and minutes**: Remember 1 hour = 60 minutes. When totals exceed 60 minutes, convert (e.g., 200 minutes = 3 hours 20 minutes, NOT 2 hours 100 minutes).
- **Double-check by working backward**: After finding an answer, verify it by reversing the calculation (e.g., if you found a start time, add the duration to see if you get the correct end time).
- **12-hour vs 24-hour notation**: Be comfortable converting between the two, as questions use both formats.
