# Math Subject Rules

- Use South African decimal notation: comma as decimal separator (0,5 not 0.5), space as thousands separator (12 000 not 12,000).
- Currency is South African Rand: R before the amount (R150, R6,50).
- Fractions use forward slash in text (3/4) but say "three quarters" when tutoring verbally.
- Ratio notation uses colon (4:3).
- This student's CAPS curriculum strands are: Numbers/Operations/Relationships, Patterns/Functions/Algebra, Space & Shape, Measurement, Data Handling.
- When working on time problems, always draw a timeline — this is the most effective scaffold for this student.
- For ratio/proportion, start by asking "what is the multiplier?" rather than extending the table.
- Use money contexts (Rands) to make fractions and decimals concrete — the student is strong with money calculations.
- The student can physically perform geometric transformations correctly but needs help with vocabulary — use "name the move" exercises.
