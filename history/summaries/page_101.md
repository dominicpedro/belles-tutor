## Key Facts

- History is learnt by using different kinds of information: people's writings, stories, pictures, objects
- Social scientists who study objects to find out how people lived are called **archaeologists**
- Archaeologists investigate the places where people lived and look for things they left behind; the things people made, collected and threw away can tell us a lot about how they lived
- Archaeologists have made a special study of the rock art that was done by the San people
- Most of the people who created the art on the rocks died long before the archaeologists began trying to find out why the pictures were painted, so working out the meanings of San rock art is very difficult
- There have been many discussions and arguments about what San rock art is about:
  - It was once believed that the paintings were just drawings showing animals and humans doing everyday things
  - Most archaeologists now know that San rock art is filled with **religious symbols and meanings**
- The colours most often used in rock art are: **red, yellow, white, black**
- When you look at the paintings, it is hard to believe that the people who made them used only **natural materials** to create the colours and paint them onto the rocks
