## Unit 4 — The hunt and the bow and arrow

### Key Concepts
- The San traditionally used bows and arrows to hunt game
- The bow and arrow was carefully constructed from natural materials
- Deadly poison from a small beetle was applied to the arrows
- Safety was important — poison was placed behind the point, not on the tip

### Key Vocabulary

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Game** | Wild animals, birds, or fish (hunted for food) |
| **Poison** | A substance that harms or kills if it is taken into the body |

### Key Facts — How a bow and arrow were made

The San made their bows and arrows step by step:

1. **The bow** was made from a straight, tough, elastic branch
2. **The bow string** was made from rolled strips from the back muscles of the gemsbok
3. **The arrow** was made of stone or bone
4. **The poison** was put on carefully behind the point (not on the tip itself) — the San took care that they had no cuts on their fingers when they worked with the poison
5. The **most deadly of all San poisons** came from a small beetle
6. The poison was put **behind the point**, in case the hunter got scratched and accidentally touched the point of the arrow

### Diagrams and Images
- **Large photograph:** A San man using a bow and arrow to hunt game, shown drawing the bow against a rocky/desert landscape backdrop

### Study Focus
- Know the **four parts/steps** of making a bow and arrow: bow (elastic branch), string (gemsbok muscle strips), arrow point (stone or bone), poison (beetle)
- Know that the **poison came from a beetle** (the most deadly of all San poisons)
- Understand **why the poison was placed behind the point** — for the hunter's safety, in case they accidentally scratched themselves
- Know what **game** means (wild animals, birds, or fish)
- Know what **poison** means (a substance that harms or kills if taken into the body)
