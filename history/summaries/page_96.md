## Unit 3 — Medicine from plants

### Key Concepts
- The San people used plants as medicine throughout human history
- The San had detailed knowledge of which plants could treat which illnesses
- San women went out in groups to collect plants for medicine
- San people also collected plants for food

### Key Vocabulary

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Medicine** | Substances used to treat illness or injury |
| **Buchu** | A plant used by the San to cure stomach aches |
| **Hoodia** | A plant the San chewed when hunting; when eaten it stops hunger and thirst, giving a better taste in the mouth |
| **Poison** | (Introduced on this page in context) A harmful substance — the milky juice of certain plants was used by San hunters to poison the tips of their arrows |

### Key Facts
- People have used plants as medicine throughout human history
- The San, in particular, used plants to treat many illnesses
- San women went out in groups to collect plants for food and for medicine
- The San used the **buchu** plant to cure stomach aches
- The San chewed **hoodia** when they went hunting — it stops hunger and thirst
- A snake bite could be treated by the San using plant knowledge
- The San smeared the milky juice from the side of a succulent/euphorbia plant onto snake bites to treat them
- **Buchu** could also be used for headaches and toothaches
