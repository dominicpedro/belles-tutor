## Key Concepts

- **Oral stories** (told by mouth, not written down) are an important source of information about the San
- Two stories are presented on this page, both adapted from *The Broken String* by Emilia Potenza
- The stories show two aspects of San life: **gathering plant food** (women's work) and **hunting animals for meat** (men's work)

## Key Vocabulary

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **digging sticks** | Wooden sticks used by San women to dig up roots and plant food |
| **gathering** | Collecting plant food (roots, berries, etc.) from the wild |
| **breastmilk** | How San children were fed until about age four |

## Key Facts

### Story 1: Collecting food (told by a San woman)

- San women gathered food **in groups**
- They used **digging sticks** to dig up roots -- one root could take about **twenty minutes** to dig up
- The men made the digging sticks for the women
- Women also carried **containers** to hold the gathered food
- The **main weight** they carried was the food they had gathered
- Small children were carried too and fed on **breastmilk** until age four
- They took the children **wherever they went**

### Story 2: Hunting song (a San song about meat)

- The San men **hunted wild animals** for meat
- The San **sang** after they had eaten meat -- this shows how important meat was to them
- The song expresses **happiness and gratitude** for having meat:
  - "We are happy now"
  - "Our hearts are shining"
  - "We have eaten"
- The song also shows the **hardship of not having meat**:
  - "When we do not have meat"
  - "My heart is sad, sick and slow"
  - "Like an old man, sick and slow"
- The contrast shows how **precious and valued** meat was to the San

## Study Focus

- Know that **stories** are one of the ways we learn about the San
- Understand the **division of labour**: women gathered plant food, men hunted animals
- Know the **tools** used for gathering: digging sticks, containers
- Understand how **important meat was** to the San (shown through the hunting song)
- Be able to explain what these stories **tell us** about San daily life
- Remember that children were carried and breastfed until about age **four**
- These stories come from a book called *The Broken String* by Emilia Potenza (adapted)
