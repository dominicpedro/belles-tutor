## Key Concepts

- **Observation** as a method of learning about the past
- The Khoikhoi no longer live like they used to -- they were forced to change their way of life
- The San still live in the deserts of southern Africa today, but their lives have also changed
- Some scientists study people who lived long ago by **talking to** and **observing** different groups of people who live in a similar way today -- this is called **ethnography**
- Cameras had not been invented when the first people lived in South Africa, so the photographs in the textbook show San people who live in some parts of South Africa today

## Key Vocabulary

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **ethnography** | A way of studying a group of people by observing how they live, by studying their customs and daily life |
| **observation** | Watching and studying people or things carefully to learn about them |

## Key Facts

- The Khoikhoi were **forced to change** their way of life (they no longer live as they once did)
- The San still live in the **deserts of southern Africa** today
- Scientists use ethnography to learn about people from long ago by studying people who live **in a similar way today**
- The photographs in the textbook are of **modern San people**, not historical ones (cameras did not exist back then)
- The San of long ago did **not** look like the San in the textbook photographs (this is explicitly stated)

## Activities

### Activity 3: Write about the San

**Q1.** Re-read pages 91-92. Also look at the photographs on these pages. Answer the questions that follow. Write the correct ones in your exercise book.

Two of these statements are incorrect. Write the correct versions:
- a. The San loved to eat meat.
- b. The San lived an easy, relaxed life.
- c. San women worked hard by gathering plant food and caring for their babies.
- d. San women worked hard by gathering plant food and caring for their babies.

*Expected answers:*
- Statement (a): **CORRECT** -- The hunting song shows the San loved meat ("We are happy now... Our hearts are shining... We have eaten")
- Statement (b): **INCORRECT** -- The San did NOT live an easy, relaxed life. They worked hard -- women spent long periods digging roots (20 minutes per root), carried children and food, and men hunted wild animals
- Statement (c): **CORRECT** -- This matches the gathering story from page 91
- Statement (d): Appears to be a duplicate of (c) -- likely correct

**Q2.** The San of long ago did not look like the San in the photograph above. How can you tell?

*Expected answer:* Cameras had not been invented when the San first lived in South Africa, so the photographs show modern San people, not the San of long ago. The San of long ago may have dressed differently and used different tools.

**Q3.** What are these San children doing that the San did not do long ago?

*Expected answer:* The photograph appears to show San children in modern clothing / at a modern setting -- they may be going to school, wearing modern clothes, or doing activities that were not part of traditional San life.

**Q4.** Explain the term 'ethnography'.

*Expected answer:* Ethnography is a way of studying a group of people by observing how they live, studying their customs and daily life. Scientists use it to learn about people from the past by observing people who live in a similar way today.
