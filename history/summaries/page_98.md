## Unit 4 (continued) — The hunt

### Key Concepts
- The San hunt required great skill, patience, and physical endurance
- Tracking was an essential hunting skill
- The poison from the arrow worked slowly, requiring hunters to follow prey for days
- After a successful hunt, the whole community shared in a feast

### Key Vocabulary

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Stamina** | Staying power / the ability to keep going for a long time |
| **Tracking** | Following the trail of an animal |
| **Prey** | An animal that is hunted or caught for food |
| **Wild** | Not tame (referring to animals) |

### Key Facts — The hunt process

1. The San hunters were very fit and had to have a lot of **stamina**
2. The San men were very good at **tracking** and an animal's tracks told them a lot about their prey
3. They had a great knowledge of direction and the environment
4. They sometimes had to follow a herd for many days before getting close enough to shoot
5. To shoot, they had to get within range of the animal
6. The **poison** worked slowly — it paralysed the stream of the animal and slowly partly poisoned a big animal, taking a long time for the animal to collapse onto the ground
7. The hunters had to continue to follow their **prey** until it fell down
8. The place where the arrow was cut out and thrown away, but the rest of the meat could be eaten
9. The San used long sharpened **spears** to finally kill the wild animal
10. They then used sharp stones to **skin** the animal
11. They cut up the meat and brought it back to camp
12. Everyone would join in the **feast**, and sing and dance around the fire
