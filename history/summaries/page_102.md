## Key Facts — How the San Made Paint

- The San painters used the following colours: **red, white, yellow, orange, brown, black**
- They used **blood** and **egg white** to hold (bind) the colours together
- White paint was made by mixing **white clay** with water
- They used **sticks** to paint the body of the animal
- They used their **fingers** to paint fine details
- A **hollow reed** (like a straw) was used to spray paint onto the rock surface

## Activities

### Activity 8: Write About San Rock Art

Students look at a photograph of an archaeologist studying San rock art and a photograph of an example of San rock art, then answer the following questions:

1. **Name some things the San used to paint on rocks.**
   - Expected: blood, egg white, white clay, water, natural materials, sticks, fingers, hollow reed
2. **Why is it difficult to find out why and how the San painted the rock art?**
   - Expected: The people who created the art died long before archaeologists began studying it, so there is no one to ask about the meanings
3. **What are the social scientists who study rock art called?**
   - Expected: Archaeologists
4. **Look at the San rock art on the left. How would you describe the shape of the heads?**
   - Expected: Open-ended observation question (the heads appear round/oval in the painting)
5. **The heads on the rock painting were not originally like this.**
   - a. What colour were the heads when the San painted them?
     - Expected: The heads were originally a different colour (likely white or another colour that has faded/changed over time)
   - b. Explain what happened to the heads.
     - Expected: The paint faded or changed colour over time due to weather, rain, and exposure to the elements

---

## Study Focus

1. Know the materials the San used to make paint: blood, egg white, white clay, water
2. Know the tools they used to apply paint: sticks (for body), fingers (for fine detail), hollow reed (for spraying)
3. Be able to explain WHY it is difficult to understand San rock art (the painters died long ago)
4. Know that social scientists who study rock art are called **archaeologists**
5. Understand that rock paintings change over time due to weather and age
