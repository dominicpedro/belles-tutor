## Key Vocabulary

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **identity** | the specific features of a person or group; what stands for something else |
| **motto** | a short saying that has a special meaning |
| **coat of arms** | pictures with a special meaning on a shield |

---

## Key Facts — The Coat of Arms

- The South African flag, national anthem, and coat of arms are all symbols of our **identity** as South Africans
- A coat of arms is a collection of pictures on a shield, with a motto
- The motto is a short sentence with a special meaning for our country
- The shield is a symbol of protection
- The pictures on the shield are part of our history — San rock art is part of the coat of arms

### Parts of the Coat of Arms (labelled diagram)

| Part | Position |
|------|----------|
| A rising sun | Top |
| A protea | Upper sides |
| A knobkierie | Left side |
| A shield | Centre |
| A spear | Right side |
| Two San figures | Inside the shield area |
| Elephant tusks | Lower sides |
| Ears of wheat | Lower area |

- The writing at the bottom of the coat of arms is called a **motto**
- The motto of the South African coat of arms is in **San language**: **!ke e: /xarra //ke**
- The motto means: **"People who are different join together"** (also translated as "Diverse people unite")

---

## Key Facts — The Linton Panel

- The Linton rock art panel is the drawing of the people in the centre of the coat of arms
- It comes from a San rock art painting called the **Linton Panel**
- The Linton Panel was a section of rock art removed from a farm called **Linton** in the **Eastern Cape**
- It was sent to the **South African Museum in Cape Town**, where it is still on display
- It was moved to protect it from the weather, the rain, and from people who might damage it

## Study Focus

1. Know the three definitions: **identity**, **motto**, **coat of arms**
2. Be able to label the parts of the coat of arms (sun, protea, knobkierie, shield, spear, San figures, elephant tusks, ears of wheat)
3. Know the motto: **!ke e: /xarra //ke** and its meaning: "People who are different join together"
4. Know the motto is written in **San language**
5. Know what the **Linton Panel** is: a San rock art painting from a farm called Linton in the Eastern Cape, now in the South African Museum in Cape Town
6. Understand the connection: San rock art is part of the coat of arms, linking our modern identity to our ancient history
