## Key Concepts

- The San as the first people to live in South Africa
- The Stone Age as a historical period
- Hunter-gatherer lifestyle: hunting animals and gathering plant food
- How the San found and stored water in dry seasons

## Key Vocabulary

| Term | Definition (from textbook) |
|------|---------------------------|
| **Stone Age** | The time thousands of years ago, during which people made tools out of stone |
| **environment** | The natural world of land, water, air and plants |
| **hunter-gatherers** | People who hunt wild animals and collect wild plant food to eat |

## Key Facts

- The San were the first people to live in South Africa
- The San made tools out of stone -- this period is called the Stone Age
- The San lived off the environment (they did not farm or grow food)
- They were called hunter-gatherers because they lived off the land
- **Men** hunted for wild animals for meat
- **Women** gathered wild honey and plant food from nuts, roots and fruits
- In the dry season, water was hard to find
- Women dug holes in the sand to find water
- They also squeezed and scraped roots for moisture
- They stored water in ostrich egg shells
- The hole in the egg shell was sealed with a grass or clay plug

- Know the definition of Stone Age, environment, and hunter-gatherers

- Remember the specific methods for finding and storing water in the dry season (digging holes in sand, squeezing roots, storing water in ostrich egg shells sealed with grass or clay plugs)
- Be able to explain WHY they are called "hunter-gatherers" (they hunted animals and gathered plant food)
