## Key Concepts

- The San and the Khoikhoi did not have a writing system -- they did not write things down
- Because they had no written records, historians had to find out about them in other ways
- There are **five main sources** we use to learn about the San and Khoikhoi:
  1. By studying the **objects** they made
  2. By learning about the **stories** they told
  3. By studying the **rock art** they painted
  4. By reading the **books** that have been written about them
  5. By studying people who live in a **similar** way in modern times

## Key Vocabulary

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **ethnography** | Defined on page 92 (introduced here as a concept -- studying people who live in a similar way today) |

## Key Facts

- The San and Khoikhoi are the people being studied in this topic
- They lived in southern Africa long ago
- They did not have a written language, so we cannot read their own records
- Rock art (rock paintings) is one of the most important sources of information about them
- There are **thousands of San rock art paintings** all over South Africa
- Most of the paintings have **animals** in them
- Khoikhoi pots had a **pointed base** (this is a distinguishing feature)
- Khoikhoi pots were used for **cooking and storing food and water**
