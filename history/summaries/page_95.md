## Key Concepts

- The San as nomadic people
- Social structure: small groups or families, equality, sharing
- What the San did NOT do (farm, keep animals, settle in one place)
- The San's few belongings and simple tools

## Key Vocabulary

| Term | Definition (from textbook) |
|------|---------------------------|
| **nomadic** | People who move from place to place in search of food and water |

## Key Facts

**Summary box -- "All San hunter-gatherers did these things:"**
- They got their food and water from the environment
- They did not grow crops or keep animals
- They caused no damage to the environment
- They made tools from stone and bone

**The San lived in small groups:**
- The San did not settle in one place -- they moved around in search of food and water (this makes them nomadic)
- They lived in small groups or families
- They had very few belongings

**Sharing and equality:**
- Hunter-gatherers shared what they had
- By sharing, they avoided jealousy
- Everyone was equal to everyone else

## Activities

### Activity 4: Answer questions about how the San lived

*Instructions: Read the text in this unit again, and look at the photographs. Answer the questions that follow.*

1. How did the San get their food?
2. What did the San use to store water?
3. Explain how the San used plants to help them in their daily lives.
4. Nomadic people like the San had very few belongings. Talk to your partner about why you think they had so few belongings.
5. Explain why everyone was equal to everyone else.

**Expected answers:**

1. The men hunted wild animals for meat. The women gathered wild honey and plant food from nuts, roots and fruits.
2. The San stored water in ostrich egg shells. The hole was sealed with a grass or clay plug.
3. The women gathered plant food (nuts, roots, fruits) to eat. They also squeezed and scraped roots for moisture/water in the dry season. They used a grass plug to seal ostrich egg shells.
4. (Discussion question) They moved from place to place (nomadic) so they could not carry many things with them. Heavy or bulky belongings would slow them down as they searched for food and water.
5. The San shared what they had with everyone in the group. By sharing, they avoided jealousy, and no one had more than anyone else.
